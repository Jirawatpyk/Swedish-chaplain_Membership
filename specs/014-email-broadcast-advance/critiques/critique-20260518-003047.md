# Critique Report: F7.1 — Email Broadcast / E-Blast (Advanced)

**Date**: 2026-05-18
**Feature**: [spec.md](../spec.md) (8 US / 67 FR / 21 SC / 10 clarifications)
**Plan**: [plan.md](../plan.md) (Constitution Check GREEN / 5 Complexity Tracking entries)
**Verdict**: ⚠️ **PROCEED WITH UPDATES** (3 🎯 Must-Address + 11 💡 Recommendations + 5 🤔 Questions)

---

## Executive Summary

F7.1's spec + plan are **structurally sound and post-audit clean** — 10/10 clarifications closed (2 rounds), 19/19 audit findings remediated (2 CRITICAL + 6 HIGH + closure pending on 12 MEDIUM/LOW), 8 contract files, 12 migrations cleanly renumbered (0127–0138), and Fly.io ClamAV deployment topology restored to dev/prod parity. The 5 Complexity Tracking entries are well-justified with rejected alternatives documented.

However, three serious concerns surface under dual-lens stress:

1. **Product 🎯 Must-Address (P1)**: F7.1 is scoped at **8 user stories** less than 3 weeks after F7 MVP shipped (2026-05-03 → 2026-05-18). There is **zero production-usage data** for F7 MVP — no signal on whether members actually compose broadcasts at the assumed rate, which segments they pick, whether the 5,000-recipient cap was ever hit, or which feature gaps members actually complain about. F7.1 is therefore prioritising **internal assumptions** + **F7 retrospective stakeholder review** rather than **real user behaviour**. The 8-US bundle commits ~250 engineering tasks before any F7 MVP feedback loop closes. Recommend pausing F7.1 P2/P3 user stories for 4-6 weeks of F7 MVP production observation, then re-prioritising US3–US8 based on actual usage data. Ship US1+US2 (P1) now since those are tier-blocking limits (recipient cap + image embedding).

2. **Engineering 🎯 Must-Address (E1)**: The **F3 contacts migration backfill (0135) is a single-transaction UPDATE on potentially every `contacts` row in the tenant DB**. For SweCham today (~131 members × ~3 contacts each = ~400 contacts) this completes in <100ms. But for the platform's future-tenant scale (chambers up to 50k members = ~150k contacts at the assumed 3:1 ratio), a single-tx UPDATE locks the entire contacts table for tens of seconds and could exceed Vercel/Neon deploy migration timeout. Plan.md acknowledges the single-tx requirement but doesn't address the **scale ceiling at which it breaks**. Recommend adding chunked-backfill logic (1000-row LIMIT + sequential pages) OR explicit "F7.1 backfill assumes tenants ≤10k members; larger tenants require manual two-phase migration" caveat in CT #3.

3. **Cross-Lens 🎯 Must-Address (X1)**: US5 (engagement tracking) is **simultaneously low product value** (default OFF, opt-in requires GDPR-aware admin action, most chambers will leave OFF — adoption near zero) **AND high engineering complexity** (open + click webhook ingestion at 50k recipient scale = ~20k-30k events per broadcast, 90-day retention sweeper, two independent toggles with separate audit trails, per-link dedup partial-unique-index). The value:effort ratio is the worst in F7.1's user-story portfolio. Recommend deferring US5 to F7.2 (after F7 MVP + F7.1 P1/P2 production data shows whether any tenant ever asks for tracking).

Lesser concerns include warning-fatigue risk from US8 PII detector (false-positive rate management), Fly.io vendor maturity vs Vercel/Neon, filename-XSS gap on attachment uploads, missing chunked-backfill for the F3 migration, and several testability gaps around the 50k-recipient pagination flow.

The plan addresses Constitution Principles I, II, III (NON-NEGOTIABLE) + V–X (Core) with rigour. Principle IV (PCI DSS) is N/A — F7.1 has zero payment surface. The 5 deviations in plan.md § Complexity Tracking are well-justified post-audit.

## Engineering Lens Findings

### 4a. Architecture Soundness

| ID | Severity | Finding | Suggestion |
|----|----------|---------|------------|
| E1 | 💡 | **VirusScannerPort isolation is clean BUT the Fly.io address coupling leaks.** Plan puts the Vercel→Fly.io TCP host as an env var (CLAMAV_HOST). Application port stays clean. But: if Fly.io changes its internal-DNS format, the operational adapter breaks silently. | Wrap CLAMAV_HOST resolution behind a small `clamav-endpoint-resolver.ts` in Infrastructure that handles both `*.internal` (Fly private network) AND `*.fly.dev` (public) AND `localhost:3310` (dev) — single source of truth for endpoint resolution + health-check. Failed resolution surfaces a typed error, not a generic socket error. |
| E2 | 💡 | **`broadcast_pii_detector_enabled` toggle is admin-convenience but the audit invariant in FR-061 is brittle.** Detector ALWAYS runs + emits audit regardless of toggle. But: what if the implementation accidentally short-circuits when toggle=OFF (a future "performance optimization" that bypasses detector when warning is hidden)? The silent-audit invariant breaks silently. | Add a Domain-layer invariant guard: `runPiiDetectionAtSubmit` MUST emit the audit event before returning, regardless of toggle state. ### 4b. Failure Mode Analysis

| ID | Severity | Finding | Suggestion |
|----|----------|---------|------------|
| E3 | 💡 | **Engagement webhook burst at 50k broadcast.** Resend may fire ~20k-30k webhook events per 10k-recipient broadcast within ~hours. Even with partial unique index dedup, the INSERT rate could lock `broadcast_engagement_events` table for new writes. | Add a webhook ingestion strategy: (1) handler ACKs immediately + queues to background worker via `Promise.allSettled` batching; (2) batch INSERTs in chunks of 100 events; (3) measure webhook-handler p95 latency under load (existing F7 MVP webhook is single-event INSERT — F7.1 inherits). If contention seen → migrate to Vercel Workflow / Inngest for webhook fan-out. Add to `contracts/tracking-settings.md § 4` and plan.md Principle VIII. |
| E4 | 💡 | **Per-batch retry consuming the manual_retry_count budget in race conditions.** Two admin tabs open → both click "Retry failed batches" at the same time. Both increment `manual_retry_count`. Budget exhausted in 1 user action. | Add a per-broadcast advisory lock for the retry action: `pg_advisory_xact_lock('broadcasts-retry:'||tenantId||':'||broadcastId)` (separate namespace from per-batch). First retry wins, second returns `ALREADY_RETRYING_IN_PROGRESS`. Add to contracts/batch-dispatch.md § 1.3. |
| E5 | 🤔 | **Resend account-level rate limit during retry.** Per-batch retry budget = 5 (data-model.md § 2.2). Manual retry budget = 3. Total = 5×3 = 15 attempts per broadcast at worst. At 4 concurrent batches with retries, the burst rate against Resend can exceed account limits → cascading per-batch failure. | Confirm with Resend documentation: what is the account-level burst rate? Document in `contracts/batch-dispatch.md` § 2 as `RESEND_ACCOUNT_RATE_LIMIT_HIT` error code. Add backpressure logic in `batch-dispatcher.ts` service that pauses dispatch when rate-limit headers indicate <10% budget remaining. |
