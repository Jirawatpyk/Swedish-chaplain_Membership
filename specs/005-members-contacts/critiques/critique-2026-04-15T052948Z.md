## Executive Summary

The F3 spec and plan are broadly strong — Constitution v1.4.0 two-layer tenant isolation is fully honoured (Principle I clauses 1-5 mapped to concrete artifacts), the Clean-Architecture bounded context is justified, and all five clarification answers are folded into FRs. Carry-overs from F2 (D1 bundle warning + US7 inline/bulk) are closed with real member counts and TanStack Table respectively. TDD discipline, coverage thresholds, and audit-log extension strategy are coherent with F1+F2.

**One security concern is a true blocker**: the FR-012a admin-initiated email-change flow (with session revocation + old-email lockout) is a clean *integrity* design but opens an **admin-impersonation account-takeover vector** — a compromised or malicious admin can redirect a member's login to an attacker-controlled email without the member's knowledge. This is high-severity for a PII-heavy PDPA/GDPR surface and must be addressed before `/speckit.tasks`. Five recommendations worth addressing would harden ops/perf without blocking the gate; three questions belong to the user's call.

Readiness: close X2 + confirm on the 3 questions → proceed. Without X2 fix, do NOT enter `/speckit.tasks`.

### Failure Mode Analysis

| ID | Severity | Finding | Suggestion |

### Security & Privacy

| ID | Severity | Finding | Suggestion |
|----|----------|---------|------------|
| E6 | 🎯 | **Admin-impersonation ATO vector via FR-012a**: a compromised or malicious admin changes a member's primary contact email to an attacker-controlled address. The FR-012a transaction revokes the member's session, marks the new email unverified, and dispatches a verification email *to the attacker*. The attacker verifies, then signs in as the member. The legitimate member's only signal is "my login broke" — and they have no way to self-revert. | Two compensating controls (either or both): (1) **Dual-channel notification**: on every email change, send a best-effort notification to the **OLD** email with a 48-hour single-use "this wasn't me — revert + freeze account" token; block the new-email verification window by 5 minutes to give the old-email recipient time to act. (2) **Member self-service email change**: for `role=member` users, offer a self-service email change flow at `/portal/edit/email` that requires verification from both old and new emails before committing — bypassing the admin flow for the 99% case. Admin-initiated email change becomes a rare "help desk" action that logs a high-severity audit event and triggers both (1) and a mandatory override reason. **Recommend (1) as a must-ship; (2) as F3.1 enhancement**. Update FR-012a + US3 AS6 + add spec § Security notes. |
| E7 | 💡 | Member self-service PATCH (FR-014) rejects forged payloads server-side, but the whitelist is only described in prose. Drift risk over time as fields are added. | Encode the whitelist as a compile-time-constant `PORTAL_SELF_UPDATE_FIELDS` tuple in Domain; the zod schema for the `/api/portal/profile` PATCH body is generated from this tuple. Adding a field to the tuple auto-allows it; removing from the tuple auto-rejects it. TypeScript prevents drift. Add a unit test that asserts the zod schema's key set equals the tuple. |
| E8 | 💡 | Bulk action has a 100-row cap (FR-019a) but **no per-actor rate limit**. A compromised admin could archive 100 members, wait 1 second, archive another 100, and so on. | Add a Redis-backed per-actor token bucket on `/api/members/bulk`: 10 bulk actions / 10 min / admin. Reuses F1's Upstash client. Exceeding triggers `429 rate_limited` + high-severity audit event `bulk_action_rate_limit_exceeded`. |
| E9 | 💡 | `notes` field (FR-001 Key Entities) is free-form admin text, up to 4000 chars. Risk: admins paste PII (bank details, national IDs, other members' emails) they shouldn't. Redaction from member-self GET is planned, but cross-admin visibility is not restricted. | Add to spec: "`notes` are visible to all admins of the same tenant but MUST NOT be indexed by the search endpoint and MUST NOT be exported by the GDPR self-service export (F9 scope — flag for F9 spec)." |
