## Executive Summary

The spec + plan are structurally sound — 5 clarifications resolved, Constitution Check 10/10 PASS, zero new dependencies, real EventCreate fixtures committed for integration testing, and the engineering pattern reuses 100% of Phase 7 scaffolding (audit, observability, RLS, rate-limit, idempotency). The plan-validation pass already closed 8 findings (1 BLOCKER migration collision + 5 warnings + 2 suggestions). What remains is **strategic-level scope and assumption challenges** that are best surfaced before committing 11 implementation days.

**Two convergent cross-lens insights stand out**:
1. **US3 (match preview) + US4 (CSV template) are unvalidated gold-plating** for v1 — both Product Lens (no user-research evidence) and Engineering Lens (extra dry-run tx complexity, 6 net-new UI components) converge on "defer to v1.1, ship MVP US1+US2+US5 first." Cuts ~4 days + simplifies architecture.
2. **EventCreate-paywall premise dismisses Eventbrite-native too quickly**. Spec § Out of Scope rejects "Eventbrite/Luma/Meetup connectors → F6.2" but doesn't validate the assumption that TSCC chambers can't / won't migrate platforms. A single Eventbrite connector unlocks auto-ingest for any chamber willing to migrate — potentially higher ROI than CSV polish if migration appetite exists.

The remaining recommendations cover PDPA Article 23 disclosure update (new consent-text capture surface), F4 cross-module failure modes, match-preview staleness, and operational readiness gaps (zero-downtime migration documentation, mid-import flag-flip behavior).

**No 🎯 Must-Address blockers**. 10 💡 Recommendations + 3 🤔 Questions for stakeholder input. The recommendations would shape a leaner, more honest v1; the questions need user data or sales-team input that engineering can't resolve alone.
