### Engineering Lens Findings ЁЯФм

#### Architecture Soundness

| ID | Severity | Finding | Suggestion |
|----|----------|---------|------------|
| R3-E1 | ЁЯТб | **Lock ordering for deadlock avoidance is unspecified**. The issue transaction now takes TWO locks: (1) `pg_advisory_xact_lock` on the sequence-counter tuple, (2) `SELECT тАж FOR UPDATE` on the member row (added by FR-037). Under concurrent issuance + concurrent archive-member operations, inconsistent lock ordering can produce a 2-party deadlock (Postgres detects + aborts one, but we want zero deadlocks at steady state). | Document a canonical lock order in `plan.md` ┬з VIII Reliability + enforce in `issue-invoice.ts`: **always lock member FIRST, then acquire advisory sequence lock**. Rationale: the member row is the narrower, more contentious resource (one per member); the advisory lock is broad (one per tenant+year+doc_type tuple). Acquiring the narrow lock first + the broad lock second minimises contention-window overlap. Matching rule for the archive use case in F3: archives already take `FOR UPDATE` on the member row but do NOT take the F4 advisory lock тАФ so the only ordering risk is within the F4 path, trivially enforced. Add an assertion comment at the top of `issue-invoice.ts` documenting the rule. |
