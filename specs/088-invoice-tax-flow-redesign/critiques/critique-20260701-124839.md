## Executive Summary

The legal core is sound and well-reasoned: killing the duplicate ¬ß86/4 by minting the tax receipt at the ¬ß78/1 payment point, backed by primary RD rulings and a validated numbering-stream separation. Both lenses converge on the same structural blind spot, however: the plan frames the work as "relabel `'invoice'` in place, ~30 files," but the genuinely risky change is that **`document_number` becomes NULL at issue for membership**, which ripples through the async receipt worker, `void-invoice`, display/serialise surfaces, and the double-mint cutover ‚Äî none fully covered by the current file list, contracts, or data-model. Separately, the **¬ß86/4 branch-render gate has no snapshot field to read** and will silently fall back to the exact `buyerHasTin` behaviour the tax-auditor forbade. Product-side, the change lacks a **rollback runbook** (Constitution Gate X), a **two-document member experience**, explicit **renewal (F8) coverage**, and an **in-flight cutover guard**. None are fundamental architecture flaws ‚Äî the tax model and numbering design are validated ‚Äî so the verdict is PROCEED WITH UPDATES: fold the üéØ items into spec/plan/data-model before `/speckit.tasks`, and gate the build on the 3 accountant fact-confirms.
